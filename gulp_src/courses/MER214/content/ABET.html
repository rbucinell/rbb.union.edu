ABET SYLLABUS For MER214:

 

Course (Catalog) Description

A basic engineering course required in the mechanical engineering curricula.  Strength of materials is a branch of applied mechanics that deals with the behavior of solid bodies subjected to various types of loading.  The social bodies considered in this course include axially-loaded members, shafts in torsion, thin shells, beams, columns, and structures that are assemblies of these components.  Strength of materials analysis determines the stresses, strains, and displacements produced by the loads.  Classroom lectures are supplemented with demonstrations.  Includes a laboratory where students build an appreciation for the phenomenon being discussed in lecture.

Course Objectives

Satisfactory completion of this course should demonstrate that students have:

  • The ability to identify and formulate elementary level engineering problems related to strength of materials in the conceptual form as well as in terms of mathematical and physical models;
  • The ability to apply the basic principles of strength of materials to the analysis of solid bodies subjected to various types of loading:
  • The ability to understand and effectively communicate the fundamentals of strength of materials and to articulate the basics concepts; and
  • The ability to effectively generalize the basic axioms of strength of materials analysis and to extrapolate those concepts to engineering structures not necessarily covered specifically in the course presented.

Topics Covered

Concepts of Stress, Stress and Strain relationships, Poisson's Ratio, Axial Loading, Torsion in Shafts, Deformation in Shafts, Pure Bending, Composite Beams, Unsymmetric Bending, Eccentric Loading, Shear and Bending Moment diagrams, Shear Stress in Beams, Shear Stress in Thin Walled Members, Transformation of Stress, Principal Stresses, Mohr's Circle, Thin Walled Pressure Vessels.
