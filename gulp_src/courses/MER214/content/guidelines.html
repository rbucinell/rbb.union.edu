EXAMS:

  1. All exams will be closed note and closed book unless specified differently by the instructor on the course web site.
  2. Dates for exams will be announced during lectures and posted on the course web page.  (Students missing lectures are responsible for all material covered and announcements during the missed lecture.)
  3. The final exam will be given during finals week on the date assigned.
  4. Exams must be contested within one week of their return.  Contested exams are to be turned in with a memo explaining, in detail, all issues that you have with the exam.  This memo should include references that support you position.   (Note: contested exam papers will be subjected to regrading, a lower score may result.)
  5. Students are NOT permitted to leave the exam room prior to turning in their exam paper without permission from the instructor.   If a student leaves without permission their paper will be picked up and graded.
  6. Individual students may request the rescheduling of an exam in the case of extraordinary circumstances.  The request must be made as soon as possible (within 24 hours of the announcement of the exam is considered decorous.)  The request must be accompanied with a letter from the Dean of Student's EXPLICITLY stating the extraordinary circumstances.  The rescheduled exam will not be the same exam given during the scheduled exam period.
  7. Looking at another students paper or any reference material during an exam constitutes academic dishonesty and will be reported as outlined in the Departmental Policy.  All acts of academic dishonesty will result with a minimum of an F on the exam.

HOMEWORK:

  1. Homework will be assigned.  All assignments will be due at 3pm on the date assigned.  All assignments and their due dates will be posted on the course web site. Assignments are to be placed in the instructors mail box in the Mechanical Engineering office.
  2. Homework problem will be designated as FORMAL or informal submission.  All homework problems assigned must be handed in at the designated time.  Homework will be graded on a √- -, √-, √, √+ system:
    √- -, -2 points, homework not turned in or little to no effort in the homework
    √-, -1 points, homework turned in but deficient in form AND results, OR not complete
    √, 0 points, homework turned in but deficient in form OR result
    √+, +1 points, homework is properly formatted, neat, all problems submitted, and all answers correct.
  3. NO LATE HOMEWORK WILL BE ACCEPTED!
  4. Homework papers are to be neat:
    Follow course     homework format for formal submissions.
    Fasten homework pages using a staple in the upper left-hand corner of the paper.
    Use 8½"x11" Quad Paper (4 to 5 squares per inch).
    Write homework in pencil.
    Use the appropriate drawing tools when preparing figures. (Hand drawing was covered in MER101).
  5. Box the final result for all homework problems
  6. Homework solutions will be posted on the web after the weekly quiz. 
  7. Each student is expected to solve each of the homework problems assigned.  Wrote copying of any part of a homework assignment from another student without referencing the other student constitutes academic dishonesty and will be reported as outlined in the Departmental policy.  Instances of academic dishonesty will result in a minimum of an F on the homework assignment.  Discussion of the homework problems with other students is encouraged. 
  8. Homework grades must be contested within one week of their return.  Contested homework is to be turned in with a memo explaining, in detail, all issues that you have with the homework.  This memo should include references that support you position.   (Note, contested papers will be subject to regrading and a lower score may result.)
  9. A homework quiz will be given at the start of lab each Tuesday.  The quiz will cover one part of homework problems assigned or example problem from the book related to the material covered.  Looking at another students paper or any reference material during a quiz constitutes academic dishonesty and will be reported as outlined in the Departmental policy.  Instances of academic dishonesty will result in a minimum of an F on the homework quize.

LABORATORY: 

  1. Lab reports are to be neat and prepared using a word processor.  All figures and diagrams in the body of the report are to be prepared using a graphics program (hand renderings are not acceptable).  Follow the attached laboratory report format sheet. (Summary, Formal)
  2. Lab reports are due 1 week from the laboratory experience before the start of class.  Lab reports are to be placed in the front of the room before the lecture starts.
  3. NO LATE LABORATORY REPORTS WILL BE ACCEPTED.
  4. Lab reports may be subjected to pier review.
  5. Each student is expected to write their own laboratory report.  Wrote copying of ANY part of a homework assignment or use of a figure from another student constitutes academic dishonesty and will be reported as outlined in the Departmental procedure.  Discussion of the laboratory assignments with other students is encouraged.
  6. Laboratory report grades must be contested within one week of their return.  A contested lab report is to be turned in with a memo explaining, in detail, all issues that you have with the homework.  This memo should include references that support you position.  (Note: contested papers will be subjected to regrading, a lower score may result.)
  7. Students are expected to come to lab prepared, having read the prelab posted on the web site.

READING ASSIGNMENTS:

  1. Students are responsible for reading the textbook, and publishers web material that is pertinent to the class lectures prior to the lecture.
  2. Reading assignments will be posted on the assignment web page.

CLASS LECTURES:

  1. Students are accepted to attend each class lecture.
  2. Students are accepted to arrive to class on time.
  3. Students are accepted to read assigned reading material before class.
  4. Students are accepted to be prepared to answer questions during class lectures and solve example problems.
  5. Students are responsible for all material covered in class lectures.
  6. All cell phones and pagers are to be turned off during class lectures and laboratories.  Cell phones or pagers going off in class will result in the reduction of the students course grade by one grade increment (i.e., B to a B-).
  7. Using computers or PDA's during lecture or laboratories for instant messaging, email communication, web surfing, game playing, or any other activity not directly related to the lecture or laboratory will result in the reduction of the students course grade by one grade increment (i.e., B to a B-).
  8. Do not bring food to lecture.
