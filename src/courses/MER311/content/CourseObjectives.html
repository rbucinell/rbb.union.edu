Course Objectives

Satisfactory completion of this course should demonstrate that students have:


    1. The ability to identify and formulate elementary level engineering problems related to the structural analysis and failure, in conceptual form as well as in terms of mathematical and physical models;
    2. The ability to apply the basic principles of mechanics to the analysis structures and failure;
    3. The ability to understand and effectively communicate the fundamentals of structural analysis and failure theories;
    4. The ability to effectively generalize the basic axioms of basic elasticity, fatigue, deformation, and buckling; and to extrapolate those concepts to structures not necessarily covered specifically in the course presented; and
    5. Use modern engineering tools for the analysis of structures.
