Journal

The course is about much more than creating a business plan to commercialize a new and innovative product. It is also about transforming you into an entrepreneur who takes control of her/his education and life to make your passion reality. Think of yourself as the CEO of your future.

Absolutely everything that we study in the context of making a product a commercial success is applicable to making your passion a reality. The analogy is to think of yourself and your dreams as the product.  The business plan is a strategy to make your dreams a success.

As you read the material, do the assignments, participate in discussions, encounter inspiring ideas in class (or out of class), keep a journal in which you transform the business context to your personal context. At a minimum, every assignment should have a journal counterpart. This includes the final business plan. You will leave this course with a business plan for your life â€“ make it a good (and ethical) one!

Your journal should be typed. Ron and Hal will collect your weekly journal entrees periodically.

The Black Quad Ruled Composition Book

You will keep notes in a black composition book with graph paper ruling. The black composition book is a trademark for engineers. It is an organizing device to keep track of important developments and ideas. There is so much important material in this course, we expect you to fill at least one composition book.

Your black composition book should include:
Class notes
Group meeting notes
Reading notes
Mentor meeting notes
Brilliant ideas that occurred to you in the shower

Ron and Hal will collect your journals at random points throughout the term. It should always be up to date.

Late Assignments

Assignments are due at the beginning of class. All assignments must be typed unless specified otherwise. Handwritten assignments are unacceptable. Assignments are accepted up to 3pm the following day and penalized one check category. The printer was not working is an unacceptable excuse. If you miss a class (excused or not), the assignment is still due. Email assignments are not accepted.
