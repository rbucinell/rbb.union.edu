ABET Engineering Criteria: Program Outcomes and Assessment

  • (a) an ability to apply knowledge of mathematics, science, and engineering
  • (c) an ability to design a system, component, or process to meet desired needs within realistic constraints such as economic, environmental, social, political, ethical, health and safety, manufacturability, and sustainability
  • (d) the ability to function on multi-disciplinary teams;
  • (e) an ability to define, formulate and solve engineering problems
  • (g) an ability to communicate effectively
  • (i) a recognition of the need for, and an ability to engage in life-long learning
  • (k) an ability to use the techniques, skills, and modern engineering tools necessary for engineering practice

Departmental Assessment Performance Criteria

  • C2: Ability to design a system, component, or process to meet desired needs within realistic constraints such as economic, environmental, social, political, ethical, health and safety, manufacturability, and sustainability. Demonstrates ability to design a system with multiple constraints.
    (MER419 Project Grade).
  • D4: Ability to function on multidisciplinary teams. Functions as an effective team member.
    (Teaming grade in MER419).
  • E1: An ability to identify, formulate, and solve engineering problems. Identifies and formulates an engineering problem.
    (Grade for appropriate Project milestone in MER419).
