1. Homework will be assigned. All assignments will be due prior to the start of class on the date assigned. All assignments and their due dates will be posted on the course web site.
    2. All homeworks are to be submitted in the following manner.
      1. A Homework Cover Sheet must be the cover page (first page) of the homework submitted. Homework not including a signed and completely filled out coversheet, will not be graded.
      2. Submit all assignments in a SINGLE PDF file on the course Nexus site.
      3. Perform work on blank (no grids), opaque, white, photocopy type paper.
      4. Scan or photograph your work into a SINGLE PDF file. Cell phone cameras work well.
        1. Minimize shadows to the best of your ability
        2. Maximize paper in the image, rotate, deskew, or crop in post processing to yield work in portrait mode
        3. Photograph in color
      5. Use the following naming convention: LastName-FirstName-Assignment.pdf.
        1. LastName = your last name.
        2. FirstName = your first name.
        3. AssignmentName = H#, P#, A#
    3. All homework problems assigned must be handed in before the start of class on the day assigned. Each homework problem will be graded on a scale of 1 to 10
      1. 1pts, proper paper
      2. 1pts, NEATNESS
      3. 1pts, header and problem description
      4. 2pts, figure/free-body diagram
      5. 1pt, coordinate system used
      6. 1pt, appropriate drawing tools used in the construction
      7. 1pt, all steps necessary to solve problem
      8. 1pt, proper notation
      9. 1pt, right answer
    4. NO LATE HOMEWORK WILL BE ACCEPTED!
      1. Homework is Due PRIOR TO THE START OF CLASS on the due date listed on the course homework page.
      2. Homework turned in after the start of class, before 7pm on the due date, will receive a -2pt deduction (out of 10 possible pts).
      3. Homework turned in after 7pm on the date due will receive a -15pt deduction (out of 10 possible pts) and will not be graded.
    5. Homework papers are to be neat:
      1. Use the appropriate drawing tools when preparing figures. (Hand rendering was covered in MER101).
      2. Box the final result of all homework problems
    6. Homework solutions will be posted on the web.
    7. Each student is expected to solve each of the homework problems assigned; however, discussion of the homework problems with other students is encouraged.
    8. Instances of academic dishonesty will be reported in accordance to the Union College Honor Code. Examples of academic dishonesty on homework includes:
      1. Wrote copying of any part of a homework assignment from another student.
      2. Copying part or all of homework solutions from other sources.
    9. Homework grades must be contested within one week of their return. Contested homework is to be turned in with a memo explaining, in detail, all issues that you have with the homework. This memo should include references that support you position. (Note, contested papers will be subject to regrading and a lower score may result.)
