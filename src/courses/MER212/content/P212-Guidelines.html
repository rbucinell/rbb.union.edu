COURSE TREBUCHET COMPETITION
    1. Competition Constraints
      1. Maximum weight used to propel arm cannot exceed 1.00kg.
      2. Weights from the course weight sets can be used.
      3. Course weight set weights cannot be altered.
      4. The total vertical displacement of the 1.00kg mass cannot exceed 12 inches.
      5. The trebuchet arm (including everything other than the 1.00kg mass) cannot add energy to the propulsion of the projectile.
      6. Before the 1.00kg mass is added to the arm at competition, the arm will be positioned in the horizontal position and let go. The weighted end must rise about the pivot while the throwing end should drop.
      7. The trebuchet arm must include a box, hook, or other weight hanging method to attach the hanging mass.
      8. The pivot point of the trebuchet arm cannot start more than 12 inches off the ground.
      9. The trebuchet must be activated by a mechanical release mechanism.
      10. The projectile for the competition will be a golf ball.
      11. Purchased kits or online blueprints are off limits.
    2. Competition Scoring
      1. Each team must present their calculations for the maximum distance that their trebuchet will throw the projectile.
        1. Calculations must be prepared in a formal homework format.
        2. One set of calculations per team are to be turned in.
        3. Be sure to clearly identify the teams prediction.
      2. Maximum Distance Thrown
        1. Each team will be given 3 trials.
        2. The Maximum of the three trials will be used to compare with the theoretical calculations.
      3. Maximum Average Distance Thrown
        1. Each team will be given 3 trials.
        2. The Average of the three trials will be used to compare with the theoretical calculations.
