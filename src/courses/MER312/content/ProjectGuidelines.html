1. A lab notebook is to be kept that is bond with numbered pages and must contain the following information for each project in this course (you can use a notebook from another course)
    1. Summaries of all synthesis exercises
    2. All calculations related to the design of the project
    3. Team meeting summaries
    4. Summaries of all outside consultations
    5. Concept sketches
    6. All activities related to the project
  2. Each student is expected to be a full participant in the project and have responsibility for an equal share of the work load in their project team.
  3. Each student team is required to develop and submit a project plan that will contain at he minimum the following milestones:
    1. Mid-Term project review/presentation
    2. Final project presentation
    3. Preliminary Design
    4. Preliminary project demonstration
  4. Lab reports are due 1 week from the laboratory experience before the start of class unless otherwise specifiecd by the instructor.
    1. Lab reports are due at the start of class or lab on the date due.
    2. Lab reports turned in after after the start of class or lab, before 5pm, on the date due will receive a -10pt deduction.
    3. Homework turned in after 5pm on the date due will receive a 0.
  5. Lab reports may be subjected to peer review
  6. Each student is expected to write their own lab report; however, discussion of the laboratory exercise with other students is encouraged.
  7. Instances of academic dishonesty will be reported in accordance to the Union College Honor Code. Examples of academic dishonesty on lab reports includes:
    1. Wrote copying of any part of lab report or notebook from another student without properly referencing the copied material.
    2. Copying of information, including tables and figures, from other sources without properly referencing the source of the information.
  8. Failure to complete a laboratory, keep a laboratory notebook, or turn a lab report will result in failure of the course.
  9. All cell phones and pagers are to be turned off during class lectures. Cell phones or pagers going off or being used in class will result in the reduction of the students course grade by one grade increment (i.e., B to a B-).
  10. Using computers or another device during lecture for instant messaging, email communication, web surfing, game playing, or any other activity not directly related to the lecture or laboratory will result in the reduction of the students course grade by one grade increment (i.e., B to a B-).
  11. Do not bring food to lecture.
