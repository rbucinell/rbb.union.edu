Class Lectures

  • Students requesting academic accommodations MUST:
    • Have the Office of Disability Services forward the instructor notification that a request for accomidation has been granted for the student.
    • Have the Office of Disability Services forward the instructor notification of the types of accomidations that have been granted to the student for this course.
    • Meet with the instructor, in his office, within 48 hours of the notificaton from the Office of Disability services to discuss the terms of the accomidation and the student will sign a learning contract outlining the conditions under which the accomidation will be honored.
    NOT following these instructions will result in a denial of accomidation in the course.
    Additonally, 3 to 5 days prior to EACH exam that will require an accomidation, the student must meet with the instructor outside of class to discuss how the accomidation will be administered.
  • Students are accepted to attend each class lecture. Missing FIVE OR LESS classes without prior instructor notification or a note from the Dean of Students EXPLICITLY stating the circumstances that caused the student to miss class, will result in 3 points being taken off the students final grade in the course. Missing MORE THAN FIVE classes without prior instructor notification or a note from the Dean of Students EXPLICITLY stating the circumstances that caused the student to miss class, will result in FAILURE of the course.
  • Students are accepted to arrive to class on time. Being late for more than 5 classes without prior instructor notification or a note from the Dean of Students EXPLICITLY stating the cercumstance will result in a grade reduction.
  • Students are accepted to be prepared to answer questions during class lectures and solve example problems.
  • Students are responsible for all material covered in class lectures, even if they do not attend the lecture.
  • All cell phones, pagers, and other communication devices are to be turned OFF during class. Cell phones or pagers going off or being used in class will result in a reduction of 3 points from the offending student's final grade in the course.
  • Using computers or any another device during class for instant messaging, email communication, web surfing, game playing, or any other activity not directly related to the lecture or laboratory will result in a reduction of 3 points from the offending student's final grade final grade in the course.
  • DO NOT BRING FOOD TO CLASS.
  • PROPHYLACTIC RESPONSE TO COVID-19
    • To facilitate contact tracing, attendence will be taken every day
    • Masks must be worn PROPERLY (covering mouth and nose) at all times in the classroom
    • Prior to attending class, wash hands for 20 seconds with soap and water. If soap and water are not readily available, use an alchol-based hand sanitizer that contains at least 70% alcohol to clean hands.
    • Maintain social distancing (> 6 ft or 2 m) between other members of the class
    • Do not share papers, writing instruments, or calculators.
    • If you are quarantined or isolate asymptomatically, you are expected to continue engagement with the course. Contact me for immidately so that we can formulate a plan to keep you up with the course materials.
